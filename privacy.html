Privacy policy

Effective July 12, 2026

What SeeWord stores

When you first open SeeWord, the game creates a player account so your scores can appear on leaderboards and your progress can follow you to a new device. That account holds:

  • A random account identifier and a username (auto-generated at first; you can change it any time)
  • Your game scores and when each game was played
  • The words you have found and when you first found them

This data is stored with Supabase, the game's database provider, and is used only to run leaderboards and cross-device sync. Usernames appear publicly on leaderboards; nothing else you do is visible to other players.

Sign in with Apple

Signing in with Apple is optional. It links your player account to your Apple ID so a new device can restore your username, scores, and words. SeeWord receives only the anonymous identifier Apple provides. If Apple shares your name, it stays on your device; SeeWord does not request or store your email address.

Game Center

If you are signed into Game Center, SeeWord also reports scores to Apple's Game Center leaderboards. That data is handled by Apple under the Apple Privacy Policy.

What SeeWord does not do

  • No advertising and no ad identifiers
  • No tracking across apps or websites
  • No third-party analytics
  • No selling, renting, or sharing of your data

Deleting your data

You can delete your account and everything tied to it (username, scores, and found words) directly in the app: open Settings, tap Privacy, then tap Delete Account and confirm. Deletion is immediate and permanent, and the app starts fresh with a new anonymous account.

Children

SeeWord is rated 4+ and collects no personal information beyond the gameplay data described above. Usernames are filtered for appropriateness.
